Correlation

A Correlation of two variables indicates that as one variable change in value, how the the other variable will change. For example Height and Weight are correlated, as height increases weight also tends to increase.

In Statistics, Correlation is a quantitative assessment that measures the strength and direction. There are many types of correlation but for starter we will start with most common,The Pearson Correlation coefficients.

Pearson correlation coefficients generally represented by rho \((\rho)\) . This coefficients is a single number which measures both strength and direction of linear relationship for two variables. Correltion values ranges between -1 to +1.

Strength: The greater the absolute value greater the strength + Extreme values +1 and -1 indicate a perfect linear relationship between two variables. A change in one variable is accompanied by a perfectly constant change in another. + A coefficients of 0 represents no linear relationship

Direction: Coefficients sign represents the direction of relationship. + Positive coefficients indicate when the value of one variable increases, other variable value also increases and vice versa. In graph, it has upward slope.

+Negative Coefficients indicate when the value of one variable increases, other variable value decreases and vice versa. In graph, it has downward slop.

Correaltion doesn’t imply Causation

Correltion between two variables indicates the the change in one variable associated with another variable. However it doesn’t imply that change in one variable actually causes the change in another variable.

Sometimes it is clear there is causal relationship. * For the height and weight data, adding more vertical structure to body causes the total mass increase. * Increase the the wattage of bulbs increase the brightness of the bulb.

Ice cream sales and Shark attack are positively correlated, but clearly selling ice cream not causes the shark attack or vice versa. Instead a third variable outdoor temperature causes changes in other two variables. Higher temperature increases more sales of ice creams and more swimmer in the sea, which leads to more shark attacks.

Fun facts:

In Regression, R squared of a pair of variables is simply the square of pearson coefficients.

Mathematic Calculation

heights = h1,h2,h3, …., \(h_n\) weights = w1,w2,w3, ….., \(w_n\)

linear coefficient \[r = \frac {cov(heights,weights)}{std(heights)*std(weights)}\]

here * cov: Covariance: Variance is the dispersion of a variable around the mean. * std: Standard Deviation: sqr root of variance
